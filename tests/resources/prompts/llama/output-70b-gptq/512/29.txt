Interest groups and lobbying play a significant role in shaping public policy in the United States. Lobbying, on the other hand, refers to the process of interest groups and their representatives attempting to influence government decisions and public policy.

Interest groups and lobbying can shape public policy in several ways. Firstly, they can provide valuable information and expertise to policymakers. Interest groups often have a deep understanding of the issues they represent, and they can provide policymakers with data, research, and analysis that can help inform policy decisions. Thirdly, interest groups and lobbying can provide a platform for diverse voices to be heard in the policy-making process. For example, groups representing minority communities or marginalized populations can help to ensure that their concerns and needs are taken into account in policy decisions.
