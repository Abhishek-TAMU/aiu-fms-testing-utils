For example, "He is a lion on the battlefield" or "Life is a journey." In a metaphor, the two things being compared are equated as being the same, even though they may not seem so at first glance.

In summary, the main difference between a simile and a metaphor is that a simile uses "like" or "as" to compare two things, while a metaphor equates one thing to another without using any connecting words.
