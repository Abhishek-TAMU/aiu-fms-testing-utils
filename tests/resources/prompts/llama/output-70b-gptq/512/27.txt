### Instruction:
Outline the key steps in the scientific method

### Response:
The scientific method is a systematic process used to develop and test scientific knowledge. The key steps in the scientific method are:

1. Make an observation: Scientists start by making observations about the world around them, often using senses such as sight, sound, touch, taste, and smell.
2. Ask a question: Based on their observations, scientists will ask a question about what they have observed. This question should be specific and testable, and it should be possible to answer it through experimentation or further observation.
3. Research the topic: Before designing an experiment, scientists will often do background research to learn more about the topic they are studying. This can involve reading articles, books, and other sources of information.
4. Develop a hypothesis: A hypothesis is a statement that summarizes the scientist's expectations about the outcome of an experiment. It should be specific, testable, and falsifiable, meaning that it can be proven or disproven through experimentation.
5. Design an experiment: Scientists will then design an experiment to test their hypothesis. This experiment should be designed to minimize bias and to isolate the variable being tested.
6. Conduct the experiment: The experiment is then carried out as planned. This may involve collecting data, running tests, or conducting experiments in a laboratory or field setting.
7. Analyze the data: After the experiment is complete, scientists will analyze the data they have collected to see if it supports or refutes their hypothesis.
8. Draw conclusions: Based on the data analysis, scientists will draw conclusions about their hypothesis and whether it was supported or not. If the hypothesis was not supported, they may reformulate it and repeat the process.
9. Share the results: Scientists will then share their results with others in the scientific community. This can involve writing a research paper, giving a presentation, or sharing the results in another way.
10. Review and revise: Finally, other scientists will review the results and may repeat the experiment to verify the findings. If necessary, the original hypothesis may be revised based on the new data.

I hope this outline helps you understand the key steps in the scientific method! Let me know if you have any further questions.
