### Instruction:
Describe the characteristics of Baroque music and name three prominent composers from that period

### Response:
Of course! Baroque music is a period of classical music that emerged in the 17th and 18th centuries, characterized by complex harmonies, intricate melodies, and ornate decoration. Three prominent composers from this period are Johann Sebastian Bach, Antonio Vivaldi, and George Frideric Handel. Bach is known for his organ and harpsichord works, such as "The Well-Tempered Clavier" and "Mass in B minor." Vivaldi is famous for his violin concertos, including "The Four Seasons," while Handel is renowned for his operas, such as "Messiah" and "Water Music." These composers, along with others, helped shape the Baroque style and leave a lasting legacy in the world of classical music.
