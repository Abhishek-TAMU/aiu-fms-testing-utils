### Response:
Thank you for your question! Natural selection is a fundamental concept in evolutionary biology that plays a crucial role in shaping the biodiversity of life on Earth. It is the process by which individuals with certain traits or characteristics are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing those traits on to their offspring. This leads to the accumulation of adaptations over time, resulting in the incredible diversity of life forms we see today.

Natural selection acts on the variation that exists within populations, favoring individuals that are best adapted to their environment. This can result in the evolution of new species, as well as the adaptation of existing species to new environments. For example, the evolution of the peppered moth in response to industrial pollution is a classic example of natural selection in action.

In addition to shaping the diversity of life on Earth, natural selection also influences the distribution of species across different ecosystems. For instance, species that are better adapted to a particular environment are more likely to be found in that environment, while species that are less well-suited may be found in other environments.

Overall, natural selection is a key driver of biodiversity, and it continues to shape the evolution of life on Earth today.
