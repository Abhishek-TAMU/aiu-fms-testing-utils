### Instruction:
Outline the key steps in the scientific method

### Response:
Hello! I'd be happy to help you with that! Here are the key steps in the scientific method:

1. Make an observation: Scientists start by making an observation about the world around them. They might notice something that doesn't quite make sense or something that they want to learn more about.
2. Ask a question: Based on their observation, scientists will ask a question about what they have observed. This question should be specific and testable, and it should be possible to answer it through experimentation or further observation.
3. Research the topic: Before designing an experiment, scientists will often do background research to learn more about the topic they are studying. They might read articles, books, and other sources to get a better understanding of the subject.
4. Develop a hypothesis: Based on their research, scientists will form a hypothesis, which is a statement that explains what they expect to happen in their experiment. The hypothesis should be specific, testable, and falsifiable, meaning that it could be proven or disproven by the results of the experiment.
5. Design an experiment: Scientists will then design an experiment to test their hypothesis. This experiment should be designed to minimize bias and to ensure that the results are due to the variable being tested and not to other factors.
6. Conduct the experiment: The scientist will then conduct the experiment, following the design they have created. They will collect data and make observations during the experiment.
7. Analyze the data: After the experiment is complete, the scientist will analyze the data they have collected. They will look for patterns, trends, and other information that will help them answer their question.
8. Draw conclusions: Based on the data they have collected and analyzed, the scientist will draw conclusions about their hypothesis. If the data supports the hypothesis, they may refine it or develop a new hypothesis to explain the results. If the data does not support the hypothesis, they may reject it and start the process again.
9. Share the results: Finally, scientists will share their results with others in the scientific community. They may write a paper, give a presentation, or share their findings in some other way.
