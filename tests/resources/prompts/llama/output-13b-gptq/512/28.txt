A simile is a figure of speech that compares two things by using the words "like" or "as." For example, "He ran like a cheetah" or "She sings as sweet as a bird." The words "like" or "as" are used to make a comparison between two things, but they are not the same thing.

On the other hand, a metaphor is a figure of speech that compares two things without using the words "like" or "as." For example, "He is a shining star" or "Life is a rollercoaster." In a metaphor, one thing is directly equated with another thing, without using any connecting words.

Here are some more examples to help you understand the difference:

* Simile: "He fought like a lion" (comparing a person to a lion)
* Metaphor: "He is a lion" (equating a person with a lion)
* Simile: "She sings as sweet as a bird" (comparing a person's singing to a bird's singing)
* Metaphor: "She is a bird" (equating a person with a bird)
