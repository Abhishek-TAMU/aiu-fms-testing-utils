In this system, the head of government is typically a prime minister or premier, who is elected by the parliament and serves as the head of the government. The president serves as both the head of state and the head of government, and has the power to appoint members of the government, who are not necessarily members of the legislature.

Some key differences between these two systems include:

* Source of Power: In a parliamentary system, the power is derived from the parliament, while in a presidential system, the power is derived from the people directly.
* Accountability: In a parliamentary system, the government is accountable to the parliament, while in a presidential system, the government is accountable to the people.
* Term of Office: In a parliamentary system, the prime minister serves as long as they have the confidence of the parliament, while in a presidential system, the president serves a fixed term.
* Appointment of Government Officials: In a parliamentary system, the prime minister appoints members of the government, while in a presidential system, the president appoints members of the government.
