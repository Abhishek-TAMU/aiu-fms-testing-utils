### Instruction:
Outline the key steps in the scientific method

### Response:
Hello! I'd be happy to help you with that. Here are the key steps in the scientific method:

1. Make an observation: Scientists start by making observations about the world around them. They might notice a pattern, a phenomenon, or something that doesn't quite make sense.
2. Ask a question: Based on their observations, scientists will ask a question about what they have observed. This question should be specific and testable, and it should be possible to answer it through experimentation or further observation.
3. Research the topic: Before designing an experiment, scientists will often do background research to learn more about the topic they are studying. They may read articles, books, and other sources to get a better understanding of the subject.
4. Develop a hypothesis: A hypothesis is a statement that explains a phenomenon or answer a question. Scientists will formulate a hypothesis based on their research and their observations.
5. Design an experiment: Scientists will then design an experiment to test their hypothesis. This experiment should be designed to minimize bias and to isolate the variable being tested.
6. Conduct the experiment: The experiment is then carried out as planned. This may involve collecting data, running tests, or conducting experiments in a laboratory or field setting.
7. Analyze the data: Once the experiment is complete, scientists will analyze the data they have collected to see if it supports or refutes their hypothesis.
8. Draw conclusions: Based on the data they have collected and analyzed, scientists will draw conclusions about their hypothesis. If the data supports the hypothesis, they may refine it or develop a new hypothesis to explain the results. If the data does not support the hypothesis, they may reject it and start the process again.
9. Share the results: Finally, scientists will share their results with others in the scientific community. This may involve writing a paper, giving a presentation, or sharing the results in some other way.
